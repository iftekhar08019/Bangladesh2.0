Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh is becoming a South Asian innovation hub, driving economic growth in technology, agriculture, and manufacturing through robust support from government and private sectors.

Sustainability

Bangladesh is advancing sustainability with renewable energy and eco-friendly agriculture, reducing environmental impact and promoting ecological health.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh is boosting its infrastructure through major investments in transportation, energy, and urban development, enhancing economic growth and connectivity.

Empowerment

Bangladesh is empowering its citizens through enhanced education, healthcare, and gender equality, fostering a more inclusive society.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Digital transformation revolutionizes business operations by integrating digital technology to improve efficiency and innovation, reshaping customer experiences and internal processes.

Green Energy

Green energy, derived from natural and renewable sources like solar, wind, and hydro, plays a crucial role in reducing carbon emissions and promoting sustainable environmental practices.

Modern Infrastructure

Modern infrastructure integrates advanced technology and sustainable practices to enhance efficiency and resilience in urban development and transportation systems.

Making money is happiness. And that's a great incentive. Making other people happy is super-happiness. My greatest challenge has been to change the mindset of people. Mindsets play strange tricks on us. We see things the way our minds have instructed our eyes to see. The most difficult part was to tell people that there is nothing wrong in making money, but there is something wrong when money making is not connected to the sense of social consciousness. Social business is about waking up to the needs of others and thinking about others as much as you think about yourself. It is about using our capacity, our expertise, and our creativity to solve global problems of health, education, and poverty. It is a challenge to show how social business can address these issues in a financially sustainable way.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
